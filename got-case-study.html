Role:
UI Designer
UX Researcher
Duration:
3 Weeks
Tools:
Figma, Notion, Miro

Overview

Go Travel: Stress-Free Group Trip Planning

Go Travel is designed to eliminate the stress of planning group trips by providing an all-in-one platform for communication, collaboration, and itinerary management. The app empowers individuals, families, and businesses to plan, budget, and execute trips with ease.

Key Objectives

  1. Simplify Communication: Eliminate the chaos of scattered discussions across multiple platforms.
  2. Enhance Collaboration: Structured decision-making tools for group participation.
  3. Unified Platform: Consolidate itinerary management, expense tracking, and logistics into one app.

The Challenge

Tackling the Chaos of Trip Planning

The process of organizing a group trip became frustrating rather than exciting. Discussions about destinations, budgets, and schedules frequently got lost in endless group chats, leading to decision fatigue and confusion. Go Travel was designed to recapture the joy of travel planning.

User Research Insights

Survey Findings

  • 70% of participants preferred group discussions for decisions.
  • 50% had negative experiences planning group trips.
  • 90% used group chats for planning, but found them cluttered.
  • 78% struggled to maintain focus in group chats.

Summary

Group chats can be disorganized and cluttered, hindering effective engagement. A centralized platform that consolidates all trip-related information is needed. In-app collaboration tools, like shared calendars and group chats, are essential for enhancing the planning experience.

Competitive Analysis

Key Findings

  • Most travel apps lack built-in collaboration features.
  • Complex interfaces and distracting layouts reduce usability.
  • Features like collaborative trip planning are highly valued.

Market Validation

Growing Trend

Group travel is increasingly popular, driven by social media trends and the desire for shared experiences. The global group travel market was valued at $330B in 2023 and is projected to reach $550B by 2032.

Rise in Post-Pandemic Travel

Between Jan and June 2023, 12 million more group hotel room nights were booked globally than in 2022, an 18% increase.

User Segments

  • Friends' Trips: Plan vacations, getaways, or road trips collaboratively.
  • Destination Weddings: Coordinate travel and communication for guests.
  • Travel Clubs: Plan group trips and share experiences.
  • Multi-generational Families: Streamline communication and manage shared expenses.

Design Solution & Testing

Key Insights

  • Improve selection mechanisms with a search bar.
  • Enhance date selection visibility.
  • Streamline booking with minimal steps.

User Testing

Testing revealed the need for clearer guidance, faster contact selection, and more detailed itinerary tracking. The "New Trip" button was made the primary action, and calendar tabs were enhanced with more details.

Results & Impact

Key Achievements

  • Streamlined group trip planning and communication.
  • Centralized itinerary and expense management.
  • Positive user feedback on usability and collaboration.

Lessons & Future

Key Learnings & Next Steps

  • User-centered design and iterative improvements are essential.
  • Streamlined communication and collaboration tools are critical for group planning.
  • Future enhancements: more detailed itinerary features, improved onboarding, and expanded group management tools.
